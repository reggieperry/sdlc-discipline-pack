SDLC pack recovery patterns + gas city boundary

A map of the twelve recovery mechanisms and where each sits relative to the gas city substrate

2026-05-25 PT · Reggie Perry

What this document maps

The SDLC discipline pack ships twelve distinct recovery mechanisms — nine periodic orders, one chain-layer defensive admit, one wrapper-layer retry, plus the post-finalizer tech-debt-classification pipeline. They accreted over ~30 minor releases as failure modes surfaced empirically. Three substantive recovery fixes shipped today alone in the v2.35.0 / v2.35.1 / v2.35.2 arc.

Two questions this document answers. First — what is each mechanism actually recovering from, and how do the twelve fit together at runtime? Second — for each one, does it belong in the pack, or is it solving a gas-city-level concern that should be upstreamed into the SDK?

The substrate beneath the pack is gas city — the open-source SDK built by Jason Knutsen and the Gas Town Hall community, extracted from Steve Yegge's Gas Town framework. Gas city provides the supervisor, the bead store, the order scheduler, the session lifecycle, the typed event system. The pack provides discipline on top: chain phases, rules, recovery mechanisms calibrated to the SDLC domain. The boundary between the two has been drawn ad hoc as the pack evolved.

Three layers of recovery

The pack's recovery mechanisms live in three layers, each with a distinct relationship to gas city. The wrapper layer wraps Claude Code; the order layer runs under gas city's supervisor; the chain layer runs inside the pack's own filing-time logic. The supervisor sits beneath all three, providing the event substrate the order layer subscribes to.

The order layer subscribes to gas city events (e.g., bead.closed, session.drain_acked_with_assigned_work); the wrapper layer wraps Claude Code at the per-session boundary; the chain layer runs synchronously inside stories.py:cmd_file at the moment a story is filed. All three lean on gas city's bead store for persistent state.

A bead's status states are gas city primitives; the final_state metadata is a pack-defined extension that the SDLC chain populates at chain-end. Recovery mechanisms intercept failed transitions and route them back into the happy path.

The canonical post-merge recovery cascade

The happy path after a PR merges, when the chain's finalizer has parked the bead at final_state=pr_open_for_human and (possibly) failed to write back the spec frontmatter. Gas city primitives vs pack policy

Gas city is explicit about where its scope ends. Three load-bearing passages from the gas city repo set the boundary:

"A capability belongs in the SDK only if all three hold. If any condition fails, it belongs in the consumer layer."

— gas city engdocs/contributors/primitive-test.md:9-13. The three conditions are Atomicity (agents need it race-free), Bitter Lesson (becomes more useful as models improve), and ZFC (transport rather than cognition).

"Go handles transport, not reasoning. If a line of Go contains a judgment call, it's a violation. An if stuck then restart is framework intelligence. Move the decision to the prompt."

— gas city AGENTS.md:194-197 (Zero Framework Cognition).

"Multiple independent observers check the same state idempotently. Redundancy is the reliability mechanism."

— gas city AGENTS.md:203-206 (Nondeterministic Idempotence). The pack's nine cooldown-triggered periodic orders are an unintentional implementation of this pattern.

Applying those principles, the eleven mechanisms break down as follows:

Gas city's scope

Substrate: provides observation, persistence, dispatch.

  • Typed events (bead.closed, session.drain_acked_with_assigned_work, ...)
  • Bead store as persistent shared state
  • Order scheduler with cooldown / event triggers
  • Session lifecycle + crashTracker + idleTracker
  • One reconciler primitive: reconcileSessionBeads() for session-to-config drift
  • SDK self-sufficiency: works with only the controller running

Pack's scope

Policy: composes the substrate into SDLC-shaped recovery.

  • SDLC-domain state (spec frontmatter, cross-batch dep metadata, retry budgets)
  • Eight additional reconcilers above the bd / gh layers
  • Recovery actions on top of gas city's typed events (drain-ack-recover, rebase-watcher)
  • Failure-mode-specific posture (HIGH-only confidence, fail-closed on destructive)
  • Provider-shaped retry (claude_retry.py wraps Claude Code specifically)
  • Operator-facing escalation routing via sdlc-notify.sh

The drain-ack-recover precedent

One mechanism above is worth dwelling on. The operator's gascity#2293 filing proposed a reactive controller-side recovery: "Issue a fresh session spawn against the same pool template; the new session attaches to the same bead and resumes." Gas city's response (PR #2380) was explicit:

"The reconciler stops at signal emission — it does NOT commit worktree changes, push, clear the assignee, or respawn. Pack-side subscribers own recovery policy (commit-and-push, clear-and-respawn, escalate) per the SDK's ZFC + zero-hardcoded-roles rules."

— gascity PR #2380 description.

That is the design statement. The pack's sdlc-drain-ack-recover order is the intended shape of recovery on top of gas city. The substrate emits a typed event; the consumer decides what to do.

Four patterns built by intuition that mirror gas city's stated principles

Several patterns appear in the pack that match gas city's design principles word-for-word, often without that doc having been read before the pattern was built. They surfaced from the work and converged on the same shape.

1. Periodic redundant observers as the reliability primitive

Eight of the nine pack orders fire on cooldowns of 5-30 minutes. Each is idempotent on its target state. Each can run concurrently with operator intervention without corrupting anything. The inline comment in sdlc-drain-ack-recover.sh:18-19 reads:

"Idempotency invariant: reset-to-pristine. Every step is safe to repeat; subsequent runs after success are no-ops."

— pack sdlc-drain-ack-recover.sh:18-19

Gas city's AGENTS.md:203-206 describes the same pattern as Nondeterministic Idempotence:

"Multiple independent observers check the same state idempotently. Redundancy is the reliability mechanism."

— gas city AGENTS.md:203-206

Same idea, expressed independently. The pack fell into this shape because periodic-with-idempotent was the only way to recover safely from arbitrary failure points.

2. Fail-closed at the trust boundary, fail-open on weak signals

The zombie reconciler ships HIGH-only v1 detection:

"fail-open on weak signals — better to leave a zombie alive than to archive the wrong spec"

— pack sdlc-zombie-reconciler.sh:25

The drain-ack recovery script lists six distinct exit codes, one per recovery step, and bails with operator-visible state on any failure. Same rule: destructive action requires high confidence; informational action allows weak confidence. Gas city's orders.md encodes the same posture in its order failure-modes section — failed orders emit events but do not retry.

3. Typed events as the integration substrate, not RPC

The pack's sdlc-drain-ack-recover subscribes to session.drain_acked_with_assigned_work. The sdlc-rebase-watcher subscribes to bead.closed. Neither polls a "did the supervisor do X yet" endpoint. The choice — typed events as a public surface, never map[string]any over the wire — is gas city's:

"Typed events. Every constant in events.KnownEventTypes must have a registered payload."

— gas city AGENTS.md:122-128

4. Drift correction over command sequences

The zombie reconciler's design intent — periodically reconcile spec frontmatter against the bead + PR layer of truth — directly mirrors gas city's reconcileSessionBeads() (~3300 lines at cmd/gc/session_reconciler.go). Both compute "want" (config / spec authoring) versus "have" (live runtime / bead state) and emit corrective actions only on divergence. The pack built spec-layer reconciliation because the failure mode showed up at the spec layer; gas city built session-layer reconciliation because that failure shows up at the session layer. Same shape, different domain.

The cross-cutting observation

Eight of nine periodic orders implement variants of the same shape: read live state from bd / gh / gc session list / gc order history, detect divergence from an invariant, act idempotently, rate-limit, fail-closed.

The pack has assembled a custom state-reconciler framework from cooldown triggers, exec orders, bash + python + jq, and sdlc-notify.sh. Gas city has one specific reconciler (reconcileSessionBeads) cooked into the supervisor, but it lacks a [[reconciler]] primitive that consumers can register against.

The class problem the inventory reveals: gas city ships one reconciler today; it doesn't ship a Reconciler primitive. A first-class [[reconciler]] config block — with a check command, an action command, a rate-limit clause, and an event-emission policy on action-taken — would compress 80% of the pack's order scripts into config. The remaining 20% (zombie spec detection, predecessor-merged defensive lookup) would still need custom logic because their judgments fail Bitter Lesson and ZFC.

This is bigger than any single mechanism fix. Worth filing upstream as a design discussion rather than as a bug.

Next-step recommendations

Strongest upstream case: order-loop self-monitoring

sdlc-order-stall-detector recovers a supervisor reliability concern — the dispatch loop missing fires. The motivating incident was the rebase-watcher non-fire that ran silent for ~9 hours overnight. By gas city's own Primitive Test, this fully qualifies as an SDK feature:

Primitive TestResultReasoning
Atomicity✅Only the supervisor knows authoritative fire-history
Bitter Lesson✅A smarter model doesn't reduce the need to self-monitor dispatch
ZFC✅Pure transport — "compare two timestamps"

Suggested upstream API: a new built-in observation order, or a [daemon] order_stall_warn_factor = 2 config knob that emits order.stalled events. The pack retains its throttle-and-notify policy on the consumer side.

Substrate case worth raising: a [[reconciler]] primitive

The cross-cutting observation. Worth filing as a feat: issue for design discussion rather than as a bug. The pack would migrate its zombie / cross-batch-dep / stale-PR / rebase orders to the primitive once it lands; alive-idle-detector and exhausted-bead-retry would stay pack-shaped because their checks are not single-shell-command-shaped.

Pack-internal review (no upstream filing)

  • sdlc-alive-idle-detector's pane-signature match relies on TUI glyphs that may drift as Claude Code evolves. Worth validating against current TUI variants before the next pack release.
  • sdlc-exhausted-bead-retry + claude_retry.py form a two-tier retry. Worth checking the retry-count metadata is observable through bd show so exhaustion is traceable without grepping logs.
